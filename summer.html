Starting a Website

This was a weird summer.

Trying to find a job was always going to be hard, but now it feels impossible. I keep questioning whether I should have stayed in college for a fourth year instead of graduating early. I look at my friends still in college and find myself relieved that I don't have to worry about potentially cancelling housing or trying to figure out to what extent the university even plans on reopening.

Still, that doesn't mean summer has been easy. The anxiety of school has been replaced by that of the job search. Eventually, I realized about half the jobs I wanted to apply for asked for experience with SQL. I had never even heard of SQL, but it seemed pretty easy to pick up.

The thing was, I wanted a place to show off any SQL projects I did in the future. Not only that, but I had a dozen or so half-baked project ideas rattling around in my head with nowhere to go. With these facts in mind, I dusted off my HTML book, sat down and cobbled together this website.

It seems more important than ever to have a corner of the internet to serve your personal brand. I like the idea that I have my own little plot of land that I can alter as I please. The lack of oversight is freeing, even if it does mean I might occasionally misspell things.

I might delete it someday. Hell, I might delete it as soon as one of my friends looks my name up and finds this and mentions it to me.

I'll keep it for now, though.

Do Rousseau's laws of land and personal property apply to websites?
